Armenian Genocide

The History

Who were the Armenians

The Armenians were Christain people living in the middle east during the time of the Ottoman empire.

When did it happen

The night of April 24, 1915 marks the start of the genocide. That night, the Turkish Government rounded over 200 Armenian figure heads in the city of Constantinople. They were sent to prison, where most were killed.

The Motivation

The ottoman rulers felt that the Armenians were infidels and they were subject to unequal treatment. The Armenians were Christians, the Ottomans were muslims. The Christians had to pay higher taxes than the Muslims and also had very limited political rights.

The Rome statute

We can apply secations A-C to this genocide. “(a) Killing members of the group; (b) Causing serious bodily or mental harm to members of the group; (c) Deliberately inflicting on the group conditions of life calculated to bring about its physical destruction in whole or in part; “.
